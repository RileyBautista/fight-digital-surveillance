Review Your Schools Contracts and Data Privacy Agreements (DPAs)

Reviewing your Schools contracts and established DPAs gives you information on what data is being shared, how it's being used, recourse in any instance of a data breach, and any (if applicable) opt-out methods

Where can I find them? (Ohio)

In-accordance with Ohio Revised Code section 3319.326, all schools would have provided a notice to students and parents with a list on all technology providers, affected records, and contract inspection information. REVIEW IT!

It's important to know what information is being shared by your school to these companies, assume your privacy is not protected by these policies, they are oftentimes vague and the bare minimum just to meet compliance. If a Doctor did only the bare minimum, would you trust them? The same applies to your privacy. Companies and schools are only doing the bare minimum at protecting your privacy

Public Records Requests are your Best Friend

If the information you're looking for isn't available on the provided information/contracts from your School, consider doing a public records request for the information you are seeking.

The Ohio State Bar Association provides a Model Public Records Request which outlines how a public records request should be created, it's available here!

Additionally if applicable, you should research relevant laws and your local School board policy to learn who to contact, what should be included, and additional information required by your local Board

Not all DPAs are Created Equal

Within my own school and many other schools within the Student Data Privacy Consortium, companies are simply using a template DPA oftentimes not personalized to the company, the data they collect, and additional important information.

This is the bare minimum, the law requires it but you should advocate for your school to create their own DPA with their companies that is stronger then the bare minimum, as of now most schools are "subscribing" to DPAs created in-collaboration with unrelated organizations and schools. The bare minimum is a foundation for companies to build on if they're able to however an insane amount of large companies have failed to protect student privacy and are only meeting the bare minimum

Advocate. Advocate. Advocate.

You can only do so much to protect your own privacy, but the issue is with the system

Advocate for you and your peers privacy, be willing to take action and voice your concern with your District and Board of Education

Change is slow, even System Administrators have issues

The Electronic Frontier Foundation published an article on how a SysAdmin at a School district tried to protect students privacy however was oftentimes was unable to make the changes in the system to protect them. They did the best they could, but change is slow. Article is available here

There is strength in numbers.

One voice is unlikely to make a difference, but you can be the spark to the fire for student privacy and against student digital surveillance. Work with your peers! Get them on-board and ask for their support in creating open letters, statements, and whatnot to get the support of your peers in the fight against Digital Surveillance, Censorship, and the continual destruction of Student Privacy.
